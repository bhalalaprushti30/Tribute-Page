Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India, The People's President"

About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002-2007). He played a crucial role in India’s missile development programs and was a key architect of the country’s nuclear weapons tests. Even after his presidency, he remained dedicated to inspiring young minds through education and innovation.

Timeline of Dr. Kalam's Life

  • 1931: Born on October 15 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960: Completed Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969: Joined ISRO and led India's first satellite launch vehicle project.
  • 1980: Successfully launched the Rohini satellite into Earth's orbit.
  • 1992: Became Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister.
  • 1998: Played a crucial role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran.
  • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India.
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Books Written by Dr. Kalam

  • Wings of Fire (1999) – Autobiography of Dr. Kalam
  • Ignited Minds (2002) – Encouraging the youth of India
  • India 2020 (1998) – A vision for India’s future
  • The Luminous Sparks (2004) – Collection of poems and thoughts
  • My Journey (2013) – Memoirs of his early life

Honors and Awards

  • Padma Bhushan (1981) – For distinguished service in Science & Engineering.
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990) – For his exceptional work in defense and aerospace.
  • Bharat Ratna (1997) – India's highest civilian award.
  • Von Braun Award (2013) – For his contributions to space science.
